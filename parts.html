Affordable Parts for Your Mustang

Quality parts at reasonable prices - everything you need for your 1968-69 restoration

Parts Availability & Cost

One of the greatest advantages of restoring a 1968-69 Mustang is the exceptional availability and affordability of parts. Due to the high production numbers during these years and the enduring popularity of these models, the aftermarket support is outstanding. Whether you need mechanical components, body panels, or interior pieces, you'll find what you need at reasonable prices.

Why Parts Are Affordable

The 1968-69 Mustang was produced in large numbers, creating a massive aftermarket industry. Competition among suppliers keeps prices reasonable, and many parts are reproduced to exacting standards. This means you can find quality components without breaking the bank.

Essential Parts Categories

Trim & Chrome

Bumpers, grilles, badges, and trim pieces are available in both original and reproduction form. Chrome work can be expensive, but reproduction parts offer excellent quality at $50-200 per piece.

Electrical Systems

Wiring harnesses, switches, and lighting components are all reproduced. Complete wiring harnesses cost $150-400, and the simple electrical system makes installation straightforward even for beginners.

Suspension & Brakes

Upgrade or restore your suspension with readily available springs, shocks, and control arms. Brake components including rotors, drums, and calipers are inexpensive. Budget $200-600 for a complete brake overhaul.

Interior Parts

Seat covers, door panels, carpets, and dashboards are all available in original patterns and colors. Complete interior kits run $800-1500, while individual pieces can be purchased for $50-300 each.

Body Panels

Reproduction fenders, hoods, doors, and quarter panels are available from multiple suppliers. Prices range from $150-400 for most panels. The competitive market ensures quality options at various price points.

Engine Components

From complete engine rebuild kits to individual gaskets, engine parts are readily available. Popular choices include the 289, 302, and 351 Windsor engines. Expect to pay $50-200 for basic rebuild kits and $15-50 for common gaskets and seals.

Where to Find Parts

The Mustang community has established numerous reliable suppliers over the decades. National parts retailers specialize in classic Mustang components, offering both original equipment and high-quality reproductions. Online marketplaces, swap meets, and local Mustang clubs are also excellent resources for finding parts, often at discounted prices.

Budget-Friendly Tips

Start with mechanical components to get your Mustang running reliably. Cosmetic parts can be added gradually as your budget allows. Join Mustang forums and clubs to learn about group buys and discounts. Consider purchasing complete kits rather than individual pieces - they often provide better value. Don't overlook salvage yards, which can be goldmines for original parts at fraction of reproduction costs.

Quality vs. Price

While affordable parts are available, it's important to balance cost with quality. For critical components like brakes, steering, and suspension, invest in reputable brands. For less critical items like interior trim or cosmetic pieces, budget-friendly options often provide excellent results. Research suppliers and read reviews from other Mustang enthusiasts to ensure you're getting good value.
