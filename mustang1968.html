1968 Mustang Restoration

Everything you need to know about restoring the legendary 1968 Mustang — costs, parts, and tips.

Overview

The 1968 Mustang is one of the most iconic muscle cars ever built. Known for its timeless design and simple mechanics, it remains a favorite among car enthusiasts and restorers.

Restoration Costs

On average, restoring a 1968 Mustang can cost between $18,000 and $25,000, depending on the condition of the car and the quality of parts used. Compared to other classics, this is relatively affordable.

Key Factors Affecting Cost

  • Body condition (rust, dents, missing panels)
  • Engine rebuild or replacement
  • Interior restoration (seats, dashboard, trim)
  • Paint and finishing
