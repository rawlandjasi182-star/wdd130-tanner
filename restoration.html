Your Restoration Journey Starts Here

Simple, straightforward guidance for restoring your 1968-69 Mustang

Restoring a 1968-69 Mustang

Restoring a classic Mustang is one of the most rewarding projects you can undertake. The 1968-69 models are particularly well-suited for restoration because of their simple mechanical design and the lack of complex electronic systems. Whether you're a beginner or an experienced restorer, these Mustangs offer an accessible entry point into the world of classic car restoration.

Why These Models Are Simple to Restore

The 1968-69 Mustangs were built in an era before computer-controlled systems, complex emissions equipment, and intricate electronics. What you see is what you get - straightforward mechanical components that can be diagnosed with basic tools and repaired with fundamental automotive knowledge. The engine bay is spacious, the body structure is simple, and every system is accessible and logical.

Getting Started

1. Assessment & Planning

Begin with a thorough inspection of your Mustang. Document rust areas, mechanical issues, and missing parts. Create a detailed plan and budget. Prioritize mechanical reliability over cosmetics initially - get it running right before making it look right.

2. Basic Tools Needed

You don't need a professional shop. A good socket set, wrenches, screwdrivers, jack stands, and basic hand tools will handle most jobs. As you progress, add specialized tools as needed. Start simple and build your toolkit gradually.

3. Create Your Workspace

A single-car garage is sufficient for most restoration work. Good lighting, a sturdy workbench, and organized parts storage are essential. Keep your space clean and organized to make the work more enjoyable and efficient.

The Restoration Process

Most successful restorations follow a logical sequence. Starting with the mechanical components ensures you'll have a running, driving car while you tackle cosmetic improvements. Here's a proven approach:

Phase 1: Mechanical

Start with the engine, transmission, and drivetrain. Get the car running reliably first. The simple carbureted engines are easy to rebuild or refresh. Replace fluids, tune the ignition, and ensure all mechanical systems function properly.

Phase 2: Safety Systems

Address brakes, steering, and suspension next. These drum and disc brake systems are straightforward to overhaul. The manual steering (or simple power steering) is easy to service. Suspension components bolt on with basic tools.

Phase 3: Body & Paint

Repair rust and body damage. The body-on-frame construction makes panel replacement manageable. Many panels are available as bolt-on replacements. Paint can be done in stages if needed, starting with problem areas.

Phase 4: Interior

Restore the interior with reproduction materials. Seat covers, door panels, and carpets are simple to install. The dash is straightforward to remove and restore. Take your time and work systematically through each component.

Phase 5: Electrical

The simple electrical system is easy to troubleshoot and repair. Basic circuits control lights, gauges, and accessories. Wiring diagrams are readily available, and complete harnesses can be installed in a weekend.

Phase 6: Details & Finishing

Add chrome, trim, badges, and other finishing touches. These details bring the restoration together. Take pride in the small things - they make the difference between a good restoration and a great one.

Common Challenges Made Simple

While every restoration presents challenges, the simplicity of these Mustangs makes problem-solving straightforward. Rust repair requires patience but is manageable with basic welding skills or patch panels. Engine rebuilds follow standard procedures without complex timing systems or electronics. Interior work requires attention to detail but no specialized knowledge.

Time and Budget Expectations

A complete restoration typically takes 6-18 months working weekends, depending on the car's condition and your pace. Budget $5,000-15,000 for a quality driver-level restoration, not including the initial car purchase. The beauty of these Mustangs is that you can spread the work and expenses over time, driving the car while you improve it.

Resources & Support

You're never alone in your restoration journey. The Mustang community is incredibly supportive, with forums, clubs, and resources dedicated to helping enthusiasts. Factory service manuals are available and provide detailed instructions for every repair. YouTube videos, restoration guides, and online forums offer step-by-step help for virtually every task you'll encounter.

The Reward

There's nothing quite like driving a car you've restored yourself. Every mile brings satisfaction, knowing that your hands brought this classic back to life. The skills you learn, the community you join, and the memories you create make the effort worthwhile. Start your journey today - your dream Mustang is waiting.
